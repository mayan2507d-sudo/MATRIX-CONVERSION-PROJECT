Articles on Measurement Units

The SI System

The SI System stands for the International System of Units. It is the standard system of measurement used worldwide in science, engineering, and daily life.

Examples: meters for length, kilograms for mass, and seconds for time.

Metric System

The Metric System is based on powers of ten, making conversions simple. 1 meter = 100 cm, 1 kg = 1000 g.

The Meter

The meter is the standard unit of length in the metric system, defined using the speed of light today.

Traditional U.S. Units

Traditional U.S. units include inches, feet, pounds, and miles. Conversion to metric is needed for scientific work.
